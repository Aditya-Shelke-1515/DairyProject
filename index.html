Established in 2001, Shivsai Dudh Sankalan Kendra has been a trusted name in milk collection and dairy services for over two decades. What began in the village of Tandali Dumala has grown into a reliable network serving the entire region.

Our Foundation

Rooted in the heart of Maharashtra, we operate from our main branch in Tandali Dumala as the central hub of our operations. With a strong foundation built on trust, quality, and community, we've expanded to include branches in Adhalgaon, Kokangaon, and Shrigonda, ensuring farmers across the region have a reliable partner.

What We Do

We collect and process around 2,000 liters of fresh milk daily, serving as the vital link between village farmers and urban consumers. Our focus is on empowering local dairy farmers by offering fair prices, timely collection, and transparent operations.

Our Standards

At Shivsai, we believe in cooperative growth. We maintain strict hygiene standards, ensure efficient cold chain logistics, and constantly strive to improve our systems with modern technology—while staying true to our roots in rural Maharashtra.

With community at our core, we continue our journey of delivering purity, quality, and building lasting partnerships that benefit everyone.

Our Vision

To build Maharashtra's most trusted dairy cooperative that transforms lives across our farming communities. We envision a future where every local dairy farmer prospers through fair partnerships, and every family enjoys the purest, freshest milk from farm to table.

Empowering Our Community

We are committed to empowering our dairy farmers with transparent pricing, reliable support, and sustainable growth opportunities. Through modern technology and traditional values, we ensure the highest quality, most hygienic dairy products reach your family.

Building Tomorrow Together

By strengthening the bond between farmers, processors, and families, we're creating a thriving rural economy that benefits everyone. Together, we're building a brighter, healthier future for our community—one drop of pure milk at a time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services

What are your milk collection timings? +

We collect milk daily from 6:30 AM to 9:00 AM at all our collection centers. Please ensure your milk reaches us within this time window to maintain freshness and quality.

How do you determine milk rates? +

Our milk rates are based on fat content and SNF (Solid Not Fat) levels. We use electronic milk analyzers to test each farmer's milk. Higher quality milk with better fat content receives better rates. Current rates: Cow milk (3.5% fat) - ₹35/liter, Buffalo milk (6% fat) - ₹50/liter.

When do you make payments? +

Payments are made monthly on the 5th of every month. We offer direct bank transfers for your convenience. You can also collect cash payments from your nearest collection center with proper ID.

What quality standards do you maintain? +

We maintain strict hygiene and quality standards. All milk is tested for adulteration, fat content, and SNF levels. We follow cold chain management to preserve freshness. Our collection centers are regularly sanitized and our staff is trained in proper handling procedures.

How can I become a milk supplier? +

To become a supplier, visit any of our collection centers with your ID proof and bank details. Our team will register you and provide a farmer ID. You can start supplying milk immediately after registration. You can also click on Be a supplier button given above and register. We welcome all dairy farmers from our service areas.

Do you provide any support to farmers? +

Yes! We provide veterinary support, cattle feed guidance, and farming best practices training. We also offer fair and transparent pricing, timely payments, and technical support to help improve milk quality and farm productivity.

Which areas do you serve? +

We serve multiple locations across Maharashtra with our main center in Tandali Dumala. Our branches include Adhalgaon, Kokangaon, and Shrigonda. We're continuously expanding to serve more farming communities in the region.

What happens if my milk fails quality tests? +

If milk doesn't meet our quality standards, we'll inform you immediately and provide guidance on improving quality. We don't accept adulterated milk, but we work with farmers to help them understand and meet our standards through training and support.
